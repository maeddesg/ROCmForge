The Silk Road was a network of trade routes that connected the East and West from the 2nd century BCE to the 15th century CE. It was named after the lucrative silk trade that was the primary commodity exchanged along the route. The Silk Road stretched from China to the Mediterranean Sea, passing through Central Asia, the Middle East, and Europe.
