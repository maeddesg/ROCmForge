 Classical conditioning and operant conditioning are two fundamental concepts in the field of psychology that describe different types of learning processes.

Classical conditioning, also known as respondent conditioning, is a type of learning in which a neutral stimulus becomes associated with an automatic (unconditioned) response. The process involves pairing a neutral stimulus with an unconditioned stimulus that naturally elicits a response. Over time, the neutral stimulus alone can elicit the same response. Ivan Pavlov's experiment with dogs is a classic example of classical conditioning.
