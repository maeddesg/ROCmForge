
 There are several reasons why your sourdough bread might be turning out too dense. Here are some common issues and solutions:

1. **Over-kneading**: If you knead the dough too much, it can develop too much gluten, making the bread dense. Try kneading for a shorter period or use a less aggressive kneading technique.

2. **Under-kneading**: If the dough isn't kneaded enough, it won't develop the necessary gluten structure. Knead the dough until it comes together and has a smooth texture.
