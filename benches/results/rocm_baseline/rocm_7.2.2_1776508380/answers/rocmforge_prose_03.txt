
The Silk Road was a network of trade routes that connected the East and West from the 2nd century BCE to the 15th century CE. It was named after the lucrative silk trade, but it also facilitated the exchange of goods, ideas, culture, and technology between China, India, Persia, Arabia, and Europe.
